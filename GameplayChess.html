Chess

Equipment

A board containing 64 squares of alternating colors.

Instrcutions

1)Sixteen "white" and sixteen "black" pieces are placed on the board at the beginning of the game.
2)The board is placed so that a white square is in each player's near-right corner
3)For each side one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns.
4) The rooks are placed on the outside corners, right and left edge.
5)The knights are placed immediately inside of the rooks.
6)The bishops are placed immediately inside of the knights.
7)The queen is placed on the central square of the same color of that of the player: white queen on the white square and black queen on the black square.
8)The king takes the vacant spot next to the queen.
9)The pawns are placed one square in front of all of the other pieces.
10)The king moves exactly one square horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
11)A rook moves any number of vacant squares in a horizontal or vertical direction.
12)A bishop moves any number of vacant squares in any diagonal direction.
13)The queen moves any number of vacant squares in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal direction.
14)A knight moves in an "L" pattern
15)A pawn moves straight forward one square, if that square is vacant.
If it has not yet moved, a pawn also has the option of moving two squares straight forward,
provided both squares are vacant. Pawns cannot move backwards.

Winning

The player who checkmate the other king.
